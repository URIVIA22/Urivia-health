Why Urivia

Built differently from the ground up

These are not feature differences. They are architectural decisions that affect what happens to your health data every day you use the app.

Local-first architecture

We designed the database layer to never need a server. Your scan history, color results, and symptom logs live in your device's secure storage. This was an architectural decision made before we wrote the first screen.

Symptom-aware AI

The AI result changes based on what symptoms you log. Burning pain and fever alongside an amber color produces a different signal than amber alone. A color chart cannot do this. A static algorithm cannot do this.

Doctor-ready export

The PDF export was the most-requested feature in beta. Patients wanted something to show their nephrologist or endocrinologist — not a screenshot, but a clean report with dates, signals, quality scores, and symptom logs.

Built for chronic conditions

Urivia was designed for the specific needs of people managing diabetes and kidney disease — not as an afterthought, but as the primary use case from day one. The condition selector in onboarding is not a filter. It changes how the AI interprets your results.
